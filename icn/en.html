Village Cyan · Culture

How might we create a shared company culture across campuses?
Facilitated by Nash
🏆 WINNER

Buenos Aires

Break silos and create a culture of flow

+
1

The Problem

Too many silos between teams and campuses. Information flows poorly, collective energy remains untapped.

2

The Solution

3 axes: (1) Centralized digital exchange space, (2) Key human moments — team building, "Live My Life" program, (3) Immersive onboarding with wow factor.

3

Concrete Benefit

Less friction, faster decisions, multiplied collective energy. More flexibility and autonomy in a trust-based logic.

4

First Monday Action

Launch a test of the new system, adjust based on feedback.

"Do we want to build this ICN where working together becomes an advantage and no longer a constraint?"
Finalist

Dublin

Implement a constructive conflict culture to transform misunderstandings into progress

+
1

The Problem

Misunderstanding between departments generates frustration, blockages, and widespread dysfunction.

2

The Solution

Regular "conflict" meetings — a constructive conflict culture. Facilitators without hierarchical responsibility, cross-functional and horizontal meetings.

3

Concrete Benefit

Reduction of burnout and turnover, increased performance and satisfaction, reduced mental load.

4

First Monday Action

Identify small issues per department, train neutral facilitators.

"Implement a constructive conflict culture."

2

The Solution

Define a common goal to unite forces. Reconnect humanity. Cross-functional restructuring.

3

Concrete Benefit

Inter-campus cohesion, shared vision, better flow of energy.

4

First Monday Action

Organize a general assembly to define a common goal.

"Create a common goal that unites all staff."

Mumbai

Integrate ATM modules into student associations through entrepreneurial pitches

+
1

The Problem

ATM (Art, Technology, Management) modules exist in academic programs but are not integrated into student associations, limiting hands-on entrepreneurial experience.

2

The Solution

Integrate ATM modules across programs AND associations. Create pitch simulations where students present projects as if pitching to an entrepreneur: financials, business model, monetization. Managed by the students themselves.

3

Concrete Benefit

Students gain hands-on entrepreneurial skills through association-led activities, complementing academic modules. Greater autonomy and accountability.

4

First Action Monday

Identify willing associations and schedule the first student-led entrepreneurial pitch exercise.

"Bring ATM entrepreneurship beyond the classroom: into associations, run by students."

Rio

ATM Act — a shared foundation of cross-disciplinary courses across the three schools

+
1

The Problem

The ATM (Art, Technology, Management) philosophy remains theoretical. Students from the three schools have no shared foundation of core courses, preventing the ARTEM identity from coming to life.

2

The Solution

Create a shared foundation of cross-disciplinary courses: engineering students take design and marketing classes, designers take finance and project management modules. Each school contributes to an ATM course cohort integrated into all programs.

3

Concrete Benefit

Strengthened ARTEM identity. Students are prepared to collaborate on shared, innovative projects. Faculty are engaged in a cross-disciplinary pedagogical approach.

4

First Action Monday

Identify pivot courses across the three schools and begin pedagogical adaptations to build the shared foundation.

"ATM Act: bringing the ARTEM philosophy to life through a shared foundation of knowledge."

Village Pink · People

How might we rebuild trust between staff and management?
Facilitated by Anne
🏆 WINNER

Helsinki

The ICN Stable — win the culture race together, not just the drivers

+
1

The Problem

Lack of recognition and appreciation. Too vertical functioning, siloed information.

2

The Solution

F1 metaphor on 3 axes: (1) The Gears — move to horizontality, (2) The Stable — recognition of ALL tasks, salary revaluation, (3) The Pilots — "Live my challenge", co-piloting and transparency.

3

Concrete Benefit

Motivation, less turnover, satisfaction, empathy, transparency, cohesion. Goal: TOP 10.

4

First Monday Action

Distribution of responsibilities — each person's place on the machine.

"Victory for the stable, not just the drivers!"
Finalist

Vancouver

Reconnect management, administration and teachers through exchange groups and expertise directory

+
1

The Problem

Lack of communication between management, administration and teachers. Information doesn't filter down, experts aren't consulted.

2

The Solution

(1) Inter-expert exchange groups led by managers, (2) Reports validated by management with info flowing down, (3) "Who does what" directory + internal newsletters.

3

Concrete Benefit

Experts finally consulted, information flowing, informed decisions.

4

First Monday Action

Create the "who does what" database + schedule cross-functional meetings.

"We have expert professors in their field, we don't use them enough in meetings that need them."

Lisbonne

Creative spaces & cross-department workshops to rebuild trust

+
1

The Problem

A crisis of trust and transparency at every level: between colleagues, between teams, between staff and management. People don't understand the reasoning behind decisions.

2

The Solution

(1) Create a creative exchange and recharge room on each campus (cushions, music, yoga) with internal communication displays. (2) Organize cross-department workshops facilitated by external people to instill a more agile culture. (3) A mini Fab Lab to boost employee creativity.

3

Concrete Benefit

Overall cohesion at all hierarchical levels. Trust is built through informal interactions and consultation. Restored transparency, sense of purpose and cross-functionality.

4

First Action Monday

Everyone brings cushions, music, yoga mats to create the first creative exchange room on each campus.

"Trust is built through interactions: let's create the spaces for them to happen."

COMEX

Student experience task force, service standardization and mobility

+
1

The Problem

(1) Students do not receive immediate responses to their questions. (2) Inconsistent standards of student experience across campuses (fragmented ecosystem). (3) Lack of mobility and insufficient connection between classroom learning and real-life experience.

2

The Solution

Establish a task force to increase staff involvement throughout the student lifecycle. Define a clear service framework (housing, sports, partnerships). Develop mobility scholarships and integrate real-life cases both inside and outside the classroom.

3

Concrete Benefit

Increased student satisfaction, institutional credibility and operational efficiency. Improved service quality through standardized processes. Enhanced employability and improved rankings.

4

First Monday Action

Identify and set up the task force. Conduct an inventory of existing services. Identify and engage interested students and key use cases.

"Standardize the excellence of the student experience across all campuses."

Village Purple · Students

How might we redesign the student experience?
Facilitated by John-Edwin
🏆 WINNER

Tokyo

KISS — Keep It Simple and Stupid: simplify processes to put the student back at the center

+
1

The Problem

Processes too long (50-page documents, 60-minute tutorials). The student is not at the centre of the system. Lack of sense of belonging.

2

The Solution

(1) Shorten and simplify processes and communication — "quick start" spirit, (2) Major competition where students use their ATM skills to imagine the school of tomorrow, (3) Invest heavily in student experience.

3

Concrete Benefit

Sense of belonging, student ambassadors, bridges between business and school. "If I tell you about my vacation in Seychelles, you'll want the same."

4

First Monday Action

Working group + survey of students, staff, alumni, professors → corrective actions.

"If I tell you about my vacation in Seychelles, you'll want the same. That's exactly what we want for ICN."
Finalist

Singapour

Project CLOSE — transform 368 complaints into 7 categories using AI to improve processes

+
1

The Problem

Student complaints are not used to improve processes. We manage case by case without overall vision.

2

The Solution

System CLOSE (Capture, Label, Own, Solve, Explain) — collect complaints, structure them with AI (368 complaints → 7 categories), resolve and communicate. Quick fixes + long-term structural improvement.

3

Concrete Benefit

Improved processes → happy staff. Reliable and quick answers → happy students. Satisfaction on both sides.

4

First Monday Action

Collect sources of complaints, structure with AI, name project champions.

"We saw this morning how we can use AI to structure 368 complaints into just seven boxes."

San Francisco

Process wiki & progress bar for student requests

+
1

The Problem

When a student submits a request, they have no idea what happens behind the scenes: lack of information, unknown timeline (days, weeks, months?), frustration and recurring complaints.

2

The Solution

(1) Create an internal wiki detailing every process, accessible to staff AND students. (2) Add a progress bar on each request so students can see real-time status. (3) Connect the whole system to Zendesk.

3

Concrete Benefit

Full transparency, fewer complaints, better student satisfaction in terms of expectations, and reduced workload for teams.

4

First Action Monday

Launch a survey to precisely map the problems students encounter when submitting requests.

"Making the invisible visible: every student should know the status of their request."
